# Layer 2: Linguistic DNA

Their word choices, vocabulary, and signature phrases.

## Analysis Points

### 1. Vocabulary Level
- **Simple:** 5th grade reading level
- **Medium:** 8th grade
- **Complex:** College+ level
- Industry jargon frequency
- Made-up words or neologisms
- Contractions frequency (you're vs you are)

### 2. Signature Phrases
Look for repeated expressions:
- Transitional phrases ("Here's the thing...", "Look...", "So here's what happened...")
- Opening hooks ("Quick question:", "Real talk:")
- Closing signatures ("Boom.", "That's it.", "Simple.")
- Catchphrases or verbal tics

**Extract at least 10 signature phrases**

### 3. Forbidden Words/Phrases
Words they NEVER use:
- Corporate speak they avoid
- Buzzwords they hate
- Overused clichés they skip
- Specific words absent across all samples

### 4. Slang/Colloquialisms
- Regional expressions
- Pop culture references
- Internet speak (LOL, ngl, fr fr)
- Professional vs casual ratio
- Age-appropriate language patterns

## Example Output

```
LAYER 2: LINGUISTIC DNA
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
Vocabulary: Simple, direct (8th grade level), minimal jargon
Signature phrases:
  - "Here's the thing..."
  - "Look..."
  - "Real talk:"
  - "Boom." (closing)
  - "That's it." (closing)
Forbidden: "Leverage", "synergy", "circle back", "at the end of the day"
Contractions: Heavy use (90% of the time)
Slang: Occasional "dude", "crazy", "nuts" - never Gen-Z speak
```

## Analysis Prompt

When analyzing samples, look for:
1. What reading level would you assign? (Hemingway app equivalent)
2. What phrases repeat across multiple pieces?
3. What words/phrases are conspicuously ABSENT?
4. How formal vs casual is the language?
