# Layer 3: Tonal DNA

How they sound emotionally - the feeling behind the words.

## Analysis Points

### 1. Baseline Emotion
Primary emotional register:
- **Earnest vs Ironic**
- **Optimistic vs Cynical vs Neutral**
- **Formal vs Casual vs Conversational**
- **Warm vs Cold vs Detached**
- **Urgent vs Relaxed**

### 2. Energy Level
Markers of energy:
- Hype/exclamation points (!!)
- ALL CAPS for EMPHASIS
- Subdued, calm, measured
- Passionate, intense, urgent
- Muted, understated

### 3. Humor Style
Types of humor used:
- **Self-deprecating** - makes fun of themselves
- **Sarcastic/dry** - understated, deadpan
- **Observational** - comments on shared experiences
- **Absurdist** - unexpected, surreal
- **None** - serious only

### 4. Authority Level
How they position themselves:
- **Expert/prescriptive:** "Do this."
- **Guide/suggestive:** "You might try..."
- **Peer/collaborative:** "Let's figure this out together"
- **Student/questioning:** "I'm still learning, but..."

## Example Output

```
LAYER 3: TONAL DNA
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
Baseline: Conversational, slightly cynical, warm underneath
Energy: Medium-high, occasional caps, frequent exclamation points
Humor: Self-deprecating + dry sarcasm, never mean-spirited
Authority: Expert who admits flaws, prescriptive but not preachy
```

## Analysis Prompt

When analyzing samples, look for:
1. What's the overall emotional temperature?
2. How energetic is the writing?
3. Where and how does humor appear?
4. How do they position their expertise?
