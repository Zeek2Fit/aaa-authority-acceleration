# Layer 5: Ideological DNA

Their values, beliefs, and worldview.

## Analysis Points

### 1. Core Beliefs
Recurring themes across content:
- What they stand FOR (3-5 core beliefs)
- What they stand AGAINST (anti-values)
- What success looks like to them
- Non-negotiables they mention repeatedly

Examples:
- "Personal accountability > excuses"
- "Simplicity > complexity"
- "Action > overthinking"

### 2. Perspective/POV
How they address the reader:
- **First person (I, me, my):** %
- **Second person (you, your):** %
- **Third person (they, people):** %
- **We/us (inclusive):** %
- When do they switch between POVs?

### 3. Contrarian vs Consensus
Relationship to mainstream ideas:
- Do they challenge common beliefs frequently?
- Do they reinforce mainstream ideas?
- How often do they use:
  - "Unpopular opinion:"
  - "Here's what nobody tells you:"
  - "Controversial take:"
  - "What everyone gets wrong:"

### 4. Vulnerability Level
How much they reveal:
- **High:** Share personal failures, insecurities openly
- **Medium:** Admit uncertainty, share select failures
- **Low:** Stay professional, guarded
- What topics do they get vulnerable about vs avoid?

## Example Output

```
LAYER 5: IDEOLOGICAL DNA
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
Core beliefs:
  - Personal accountability > excuses
  - Simplicity > complexity
  - Action > overthinking
  - Faith/values first (if applicable)
Stands against: Victimhood mentality, overcomplicated "guru" systems
POV: 80% second-person (you), 15% first-person (I), 5% we/us
Contrarian: High - frequently challenges mainstream advice
Vulnerability: Medium - shares failures but not deep personal trauma
```

## Analysis Prompt

When analyzing samples, look for:
1. What beliefs repeat across multiple pieces?
2. How do they address the reader?
3. Do they challenge conventional wisdom?
4. How personally vulnerable do they get?
