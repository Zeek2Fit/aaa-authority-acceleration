# Disgust Categories Reference

Summary of all 10 categories and their purpose.

---

## Category 1: Style Archetypes
**Questions 1-2 | High Impact**

What writing "voices" they hate:
- Corporate jargon
- Academic speak
- Victorian prose
- Gen-Z slang
- Self-help guru
- Clickbait sensational

**Use for:** Blocking entire voice archetypes

---

## Category 2: Taboo Topics
**Questions 3-4 | Critical**

Absolute boundaries:
- Political stance (never touch vs lean vs hot takes)
- Profanity level (none to unfiltered)

**Use for:** Hard guardrails that should NEVER be violated

---

## Category 3: Emotional Tones
**Questions 5-6 | High Impact**

Emotional registers that feel wrong:
- Toxic positivity
- Cynical snark
- Preachy moralizing
- Victim mentality
- Earnest vs ironic spectrum

**Use for:** Setting emotional boundaries for all content

---

## Category 4: Structural Hates
**Questions 7-8 | Medium Impact**

Structural patterns that trigger them:
- Passive voice overload
- Choppy fragments
- Run-on sentences
- Question spam
- Emoji overload
- CAPS LOCK yelling
- Buzzword bingo
- Cliche overload

**Use for:** Editing and structural checks

---

## Category 5: Personal Quirks
**Questions 9-10 | Lower Impact but Personal**

Individual pet peeves:
- Metaphor type aversions (sports, food, war, etc.)
- Grammar/style pet peeves (So..., honestly, etc.)

**Use for:** Fine-tuning and personal authenticity

---

## Priority Weighting

When resolving conflicts, prioritize by:

1. **Category 2: Taboo Topics** (Score: 1.5x)
   - These are ABSOLUTE boundaries

2. **Category 3: Emotional Tones** (Score: 1.25x)
   - Gets to core authenticity

3. **Category 1: Style Archetypes** (Score: 1.0x)
   - Important but can flex slightly

4. **Category 4: Structural Hates** (Score: 0.75x)
   - More about polish than authenticity

5. **Category 5: Personal Quirks** (Score: 0.5x)
   - Nice to have, not critical
